## The three findings that matter

### 1. CTR is your fastest revenue lever — you're being seen, not clicked
27,802 impressions and only 152 clicks. A 0.55% sitewide CTR at an average position of ~9 is far below normal (position 8–10 typically earns 1.5–2.5%). You don't have a visibility problem on your blog content — you have a **title/snippet problem**.

The clearest example: **`/blog/golden-retriever-lifetime-costs`** pulled **10,822 impressions but just 31 clicks (0.29% CTR)** at position 8.2. At a normal 2% CTR that page alone would earn ~216 clicks/28 days instead of 31 — roughly **+185 clicks** from title/meta work on a single page. Apply better titles across the top 6 blog pages and a 0.55% → 2% sitewide CTR would mean ~550 clicks vs 152 (≈3.6×) with **zero new content or links**.

### 2. The indexing problem is confirmed and severe — only 24 URLs get impressions
Out of 1,100+ URLs in your sitemap, **only 24 pages receive any impressions, and every one of them is a `/blog/` article or the homepage.** Your 300+ breed pages, ~750 compare pages, and regional cost pages are essentially **invisible in search.** This validates the CEO report's #1 risk: the programmatic templates aren't earning traffic. Fixing thin-content/indexing on those templates is where the long-term traffic ceiling lifts.

### 3. The bright spot: "pet cost calculator" — position 4, 22.8% CTR
Your core tool term **`pet cost calculator`** got 13 clicks from 57 impressions (**22.81% CTR at position 4.1**) — by far your best query. The brand/tool cluster converts. Pushing this from position 4 into the top 3 (internal links, homepage title tuning, a few relevant backlinks — which the outreach task is already chasing) compounds directly.
